High-level hero logic

  • Do not build every legendary equally just because it looks powerful.
  • Value heroes by role fit, march purpose, and future event alignment.
  • Save premium pulls for the events or banners that actually change your account.
  • Think in core lineups plus supports, not in random collection progress.

Where players usually make mistakes

  • Overspending gems during weak value windows.
  • Splitting fragments too broadly.
  • Copying whale lineups without the same investment depth.
  • Ignoring generation timing and upcoming event rotations.

Fast pull rule

If a hero does not improve your main march, your event score, or your next meaningful combat breakpoint, it is usually not the right place to spend premium resources. The strongest accounts are not the ones that touch everything. They are the ones that keep saying no until one upgrade really changes the account.

Three questions before you pull

  1. Does this hero solve a real lineup weakness or just look exciting?
  2. Will this spend happen inside a stronger event window than waiting would?
  3. Can your account actually support this hero with fragments, gear, and march context?
